It's not linear — it loops

Draw the arrows and the "process" collapses into a few tight feedback loops. These are where buyers get stuck, re-decide, and abandon.

Budget ↔ Market reality
Every car at my budget looks suspicious, so I stretch the budget — and the next tier looks suspicious too. Buyers loop here for weeks before accepting a compromise.
Shortlist ↔ Stale / fake listing
Message the seller → dead number, sold long ago, or bait price. Back to scrolling. Each dead end resets the hunt and burns trust in every platform.
Trust ↔ Inspection cost
Verifying each candidate costs $40 and half a day, so after car #3 buyers stop verifying — exactly when they're most tired and most likely to get burned.

The biggest insight: the buyer's real job isn't choosing a car — it's surviving a hostile information environment. "Qualify me once, then hunt and verify for me" deletes the entire two-week scroll-and-call grind, and that whole flow lives in chat with no government integration needed.
