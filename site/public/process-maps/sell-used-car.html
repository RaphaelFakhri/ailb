It's not linear — it loops

Draw the arrows and the "process" collapses into a few tight feedback loops. These are where sellers get stuck, re-decide, and abandon.

Price ↔ Silence ↔ Lowballers
No bites, so I drop the price — and the lowballers re-anchor even lower. With no real market data, every drop feels like panic, not strategy.
Listing ↔ Buried ↔ Repost
Every platform buries you in 48 hours. Sellers bump, repost, and pay for features in a cycle that resets weekly — until they give up or dump the price.
"Serious buyer" ↔ No-show ↔ Re-screen
Each no-show burns an afternoon and erodes trust, so I screen harder — and scare away real buyers. Many sellers quit here and sell cheap to a dealer.

The wall is dripping red: pricing blind, stranger danger, counterfeit fear, and a state-run transfer at the end. The agent can collapse the marketplace half beautifully — but the mécanique/daftar transfer is a physical, government-bound step we can checklist, not delete, and people sell a car only every few years.
