What the wall tells us

Patterns that emerge when you read all 15 maps side by side — use these lines in the pitch.

The common enemy is trust, not search

Across all 15 maps the red stickies are the same species: no prices, no reviews, no escrow, no recourse, strangers and cash. Lebanon doesn't lack marketplaces — it lacks a trust layer. ailb's agent is that layer: it qualifies, verifies, and vouches.

The interview replaces the platform

Every winning flow starts the same way: the agent asks you about yourself once — what you're selling, what you can do, where you want to go — and the listing, profile, or document file falls out of the conversation. No forms anywhere.

The moonshot story: car A→Z

Map #8 (sell car) + #5 (buy car) + #14 (transfer) chain into one narrative: ailb sells your car, finds the buyer their car, and walks both through the mécanique with a prepared file and one visit. Pitch it as the vision; demo the trio above as the proof.

Government = concierge, not integration

No API will save you at the mukhtar. The honest gov play is knowing the live requirements, prefilling the file, watching validity clocks (ikhraj qaid < 3 months!), sequencing dependencies, and making every mandatory visit a single prepared one.
